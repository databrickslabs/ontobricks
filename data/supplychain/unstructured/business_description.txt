Internal Business Description Document
Supply Chain Operations & Data Model

================================================================================
1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

GlobalTrade Logistics Corp (GTL) is a multinational manufacturing and distribution company operating across six continents. Founded in 1995, the company has grown from a regional parts distributor into an integrated supply chain operator managing the full lifecycle of products from raw material sourcing through to end-customer delivery. Our core business spans three primary segments: industrial components, consumer goods, and specialty chemicals. We maintain strategic partnerships with over 60 suppliers worldwide and serve approximately 150 active customers ranging from retail chains and wholesalers to original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and e-commerce platforms.

The company operates a network of 10 distribution centers and fulfillment warehouses strategically located in major trade hubs including Shanghai, Rotterdam, Singapore, Dubai, Los Angeles, Hamburg, Tokyo, Mumbai, Antwerp, and Busan. Each facility is classified by function—distribution centers handle bulk replenishment, fulfillment centers process direct-to-consumer orders, cold storage facilities manage temperature-sensitive products, transit hubs facilitate cross-docking, and regional DCs serve local market demand. Total warehouse capacity exceeds 250,000 square meters.

================================================================================
2. SUPPLIER RELATIONSHIPS AND PROCUREMENT
================================================================================

Supplier management is a cornerstone of our operations. We maintain a curated supplier base of 60 approved vendors, each evaluated on quality, reliability, pricing, and geographic coverage. Each supplier is assigned a performance rating (3.0 to 5.0) based on on-time delivery, quality inspection results, and responsiveness. Suppliers are categorized by product category—Electronics, Raw Materials, Packaging, Mechanical Parts, Chemicals, Textiles, Automotive, Consumer Goods, Industrial Equipment, Food & Beverage, Pharmaceuticals, and Semiconductors. Our procurement team uses these ratings to prioritize orders and negotiate terms.

Purchase orders are the primary mechanism for replenishment. A typical PO flows through statuses: draft, submitted, approved, in_transit, received, or cancelled. Each PO references a single supplier and includes order date, expected delivery date, total amount, and line items (product, quantity, unit price, line total). We process approximately 400 purchase orders per quarter, with order values ranging from $500 to $150,000. Order lines capture the granular product-level detail—typically 3 lines per PO on average, totaling around 1,200 order lines per quarter.

================================================================================
3. WAREHOUSE MANAGEMENT AND INVENTORY
================================================================================

Inventory is tracked at the product-warehouse level. Each combination of product and warehouse has a single inventory record capturing quantity on hand, reorder level, and last restock date. We maintain approximately 500 active inventory positions across our 10 warehouses and 100 product SKUs. Products are organized into 12 categories with optional parent-child hierarchy (e.g., Automotive is a subcategory of Mechanical Parts, Semiconductors under Electronics).

Reorder levels are set per product-warehouse based on historical demand and lead times. When quantity on hand falls at or below reorder level, the system triggers replenishment workflows. Last restock date supports cycle counting and inventory aging analysis. Our warehouse management system (WMS) integrates with the inventory data to drive picking, packing, and putaway operations.

================================================================================
4. INBOUND LOGISTICS: SHIPMENTS AND DELIVERY EVENTS
================================================================================

When a purchase order is fulfilled by a supplier, the goods are shipped to one of our warehouses. Each shipment links a PO to a destination warehouse and captures carrier (FedEx, DHL, Maersk, UPS, CMA CGM, MSC, Kuehne+Nagel, DB Schenker, XPO Logistics, CEVA), tracking number, ship date, estimated arrival, and status (pending, picked_up, in_transit, delivered, delayed, exception). We process roughly 600 shipments per quarter.

Delivery events provide granular visibility into shipment progress. Each event has a type (pickup, departure, arrival, customs_clearance, delivery, exception, delay), timestamp, location, and description. We record approximately 1,500 delivery events per quarter, enabling real-time tracking and exception management. This data feeds our customer-facing tracking portal and internal logistics dashboards.

================================================================================
5. QUALITY CONTROL
================================================================================

Quality inspections are performed on incoming shipments. Each inspection is linked to a shipment and product, and records inspection date, inspector ID, result (passed, failed, conditional, pending_review), defect count, and notes. We conduct approximately 300 inspections per quarter, with failed or conditional results triggering quarantine and supplier notification workflows. Defect counts support supplier scorecard calculations and root cause analysis.

================================================================================
6. SALES AND OUTBOUND DISTRIBUTION
================================================================================

Sales orders are placed by customers and fulfilled from our warehouse network. Each sales order references a customer and a fulfilling warehouse. We serve 150 customers across types: retail, wholesale, distributor, manufacturer, and ecommerce. Customer credit limits are maintained to support order approval workflows. We process approximately 500 sales orders per quarter with order values from $100 to $75,000.

Sales order lines capture product, quantity, unit price, and line total. We average 3 lines per sales order, totaling around 1,500 lines per quarter. Order status flows: draft, confirmed, picking, shipped, delivered, cancelled. Ship date is recorded when the order leaves the warehouse.

================================================================================
7. RETURNS MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

Return requests are initiated by customers and linked to the original sales order. Each return has a request date, reason (defective, wrong_item, damaged_in_transit, customer_change, quality_issue, expired), status (requested, approved, received, refunded, rejected), and refund amount. We process approximately 200 return requests per quarter. This data supports reverse logistics planning, supplier quality feedback, and customer satisfaction analysis.

================================================================================
8. DATA INTEGRATION AND USE CASES
================================================================================

The supply chain data model supports end-to-end visibility from supplier to customer. Key analytical use cases include:

- Order fulfillment tracking: PO → Shipment → Delivery Event → Warehouse receipt
- Inventory optimization: Stock levels, reorder triggers, restock frequency by product and warehouse
- Supplier performance: Spend, order count, delivery reliability, quality metrics
- Customer analytics: Order patterns, return rates, credit utilization
- Logistics efficiency: Carrier performance, transit times, exception rates

All entities are explicitly connected through foreign keys, enabling integrated reporting and ontology-based knowledge graph construction for semantic querying and AI-driven insights.
